From the figures above, we can see that, total grade of E school follows normal distribution well, while D school are not well normally distributed. From figure of Summary, we can see that the average grade is a little different. To be more precise, the mean total grade of D school and E school are 574.4462, 599.2185. Then, are the total grades of these two school differ from each other? Letâ€™s check it with t test.
Null hypothesis (H0) is: no difference between the two means.
And the alternate hypothesis (H1) is: two samples are different from each other.
Then, the result of Welch Two Sample t-test is:

P value of Welch Two Sample t-test is 8.5966 Ã— 10-11, which is much smaller than 0.05. The probability of Type I Error is very small. It means, our null hypothesis should be rejected. Therefore, there is a siginificant difference between D and E school.
Then, is that reliable that the mean total grade of E school is greater than that of D school? We still use Welch Two Sample t-test.

From t.test above, P value is 1, which is very high. It means probability of Type I Error is high. So we cannot reject null hypothesis. But it does not mean we can accept null hypothesis. In fact, it only suggests that there is not sufficient evidence against null hypothesis in favour of alternative hypothesis. Therefore, we only know grade of Chinese and English are different.

  • Math and Physics

Green lines in the figure above, means 60% of the maximum possible grade, i.e.Â the pass grade. And blue llines means 95% of the maximum possible grade, i.e.Â the excellent grade. We can see that, most students passed the exam of Math and Physics. But only a few students get the excellent grade.

When solving physical problems, it is inevitable to use mathematical skills. Then, can we get a conclusion that higher Physics grade depends on higher Math grade? We can test this by testing if there exist a positive linear correlation between Math grade and Physics grade.
Are Math and Physics grade normally distributed? We need to check this first.

From figures above, we can see that Math grade is well normally distributed. But Physics grade is roughly normal distributed. Then, we can use Pearson's product-moment correlation test.
If the alternative hypothesis (H1) is: the grade of Physics is positively associated with the grade of Math. Then the test result is:

## 
##  Pearson's product-moment correlation
## 
## data:  e.phys and e.math
## t = 30.46, df = 1147, p-value < 2.2e-16
## alternative hypothesis: true correlation is greater than 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  0.6409 1.0000
## sample estimates:
##    cor 
## 0.6687

From Pearson's product-moment correlation test, we get P value equals 0. This small P value means we should reject our null hypothesis, and the alternative hypothesis might be true. And the Pearson product-moment correlation coefficient is 0.669. Therefore, there might be a positive correlation between Math and Physics grade.
Then we can find is there a linear regression between the two subjects.

From the analysis above, we know that there exists a linear regression, with intercept of -4.9655 and slope of 0.6369. From the figure above, we can see that students with higher Physics grade also have higher Math grade. And there are still some students who have higher Math grade, but relative low Physics grade. Therefore, good Math skill does contribute to higher Physics, but it is not a rule.
